Welcome to the City of Monash

The City of Monash is a local government area in Victoria that is located in the south - eastern side of Melbourne. It was established in 1984 and has an estimated population of around 200,000 as of 2024. Its council’s office is in Glen Waverley.

It got its name after Sir John Monash, a famous general and engineer. It’s home to Monash University, John Monash Science school and Monash Medical centre. It’s considered a major employment hub for medicine and education.

The City of Monash is regarded as one the most liveable cities in Victoria however, is that true with its questionable road safety?

City of Monash vs other Local Governments

This graph shows that the City of Monash has the 7th most number of road accidents in Victoria, with 5743 road accidents in 2024. The City of Melbourne ranks the highest in Victoria with 10005 road accidents, while the Shire of Yarra Ranges ranks 10th with 5228 road accidents, placing Monash to the lower end of the graph.

This shows that the City of Monash is considered one of the most dangerous cities in terms of road safety. However, this may not be entirely the case. (Read other pages for more information about the state of City of Monash’s road safety).

Conclusion

The City of Monash has been able to implement a degree of infrastructure such as foot paths and bike lanes to ensure that no major road incidents occur. However, due to their lack of maintaining their roads, the number of road accidents occurring has risen especially in more populated roads and more common speed zones. Not to mention how the City of Monash has not implemented major road regulations as of 2021, it's evident why the City of Monash is one of the most dangerous cities in Victoria in road safety.
